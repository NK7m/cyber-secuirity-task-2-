INTERVIEW QUESTIONS ANSWERS:

1. What is phishing?
Phishing is a cyber attack that uses disguised email, text, or website as a weapon to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.

2. How to identify a phishing email?
Look for suspicious sender addresses, urgent/threatening language, mismatched URLs, poor grammar, requests for sensitive info, and unexpected attachments.

3. What is email spoofing?
Email spoofing is forging the sender's address to make it appear the email comes from a legitimate source when it's actually from an attacker.

4. Why are phishing emails dangerous?
They can lead to identity theft, financial loss, malware infections, and unauthorized access to personal or corporate systems.

5. How can you verify the sender's authenticity?
Check email headers, verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC records, contact the organization through official channels, and look for digital signatures.

6. What tools can analyze email headers?
MXToolbox, Google Admin Toolbox Messageheader, Microsoft Message Header Analyzer, and various email client header viewers.

7. What actions should be taken on suspected phishing emails?
Don't click links/download attachments. Report to IT/security team. Forward to anti-phishing organizations. Delete after reporting.

8. How do attackers use social engineering in phishing?
They exploit human psychology by creating urgency, impersonating authority figures, offering fake rewards, or using fear tactics to bypass logical thinking.
